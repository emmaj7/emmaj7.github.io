Trip to Mars, 2019

Research Engineer @ Smart Mobility Lab, KTH
Trip to Mars is a block programming game, which purpose is to introduce programming to children in 7th grade. The game was developed during the summer of 2019, and presented at the Museum of Technology, Stockholm, in September the same year. Curious about the game and the exhibition?
Collaborator: Mikael Glamheden

Tame Impala Sign-up page, 2020

Coursework, UX design and evaluation
This prototype was developed during a 10-hour challenge where I was prompted to design for a frictionless sign-up process using Figma. I chose to work with Tame Impala in an attempt to reflect Kevin Parker's music in my design. Pro tip:set the mood before diving into the prototype.

Stockholm Sound Map, 2020

Coursework, Sound in Interaction
The Stockholm Sound Map is a sonic deptiction of a dystopian future caused by climate change. The interaction is based on a motion capture system and programmed in Max 8. Are you ready to check it out?
Collaborator: Mikaela Persson

Jump Startr, 2020

Coursework, Physical Interaction and Realization
Jump Startr is so much more than a trampoline - it is a human powered party! By interacting with Jump Startr your energy is harvested in order for lights and music to do their thing.
Collaborators: Tintin Torin, Mikaela Persson, Lovisa Carlberg, Kajsa Saare
